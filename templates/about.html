About The Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Hardoi

Establishment

As per the policy of the Government, one Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya is to be established in each district. Accordingly, 62 new JNVs have been sanctioned during 2016-17 in various districts of the country. In addition to this,10 JNVs each have been sanctioned in districts having a large concentration of SC & ST population. Besides this, 3 JNVs have been sanctioned in 03 districts i.e. Senapati-II (Manipur), Ukhrul-II (Manipur) & Ratlam-II (Madhya Pradesh) as special case, bringing the total number of JNVs sanctioned to 661. Each Vidyalaya has provision for a full-fledged campus with sufficient building for classrooms, dormitories, staff-quarters, dining-hall and other infrastructural facilities viz. play-grounds, workshops, library and labs etc. The opening of new Navodaya Vidyalayas is based on the offer from the State Government to provide around 30 acres of suitable land (relaxable on case to case basis) free of cost, alongwith sufficient temporary accommodation to run the school till the completion of permanent Vidyalaya building.


Overview

Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Hardoi, is a fully residential, co-educational school affiliated to CBSE, New Delhi was established in 1992 and has classes from VI to XII standard. This Vidyalaya is run by Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti, New Delhi, an autonomous body under the Department of Education and Literacy, Ministry of Education formely known as Ministry of Human Resource and Development(1985-2020), Government of India.

The school campus covers around 39.8 acres of forested land having various Buildings ( Administration & Academic Block, Hostels, Mess, Staff Quarters, Special Multi-purpose Hall [Under Construction]) and Playgrounds.


Administration

For each JNV, there is a Vidyalaya Advisory Committee for assistance on matters of academics, infrastructure and other general activities and a Vidyalaya Management Committee for budget preparation, selection of ad-hoc teachers and proper functioning of the school.
Normally the district collector of the concerned district is the ex-officio chairman of school level committees with local educationists, public representatives and officers from the district as members.

Mess

Mess is an integral part of JNVs having modern and clean kitchen which provides free of cost fooding facility to all students.The food court kitchen is equipped with highly sophisticated cooking equipment, provides hygienic, delicious and wholesome food, prepared under the direction of well qualified and experienced catering staff. It has a common dinning hall, for boys, girls and teachers. The ambience of the mess is so quite. Students used to do "Bhojan Mantra" before having the meal.

The dining hall is well ventilated, decorated and delightfully arranged. Audio visual equipments are also fitted in the hall to entertain the students as well as the information update. There is a tender system for the procurement of raw materials. Indents are placed to the suppliers well in advance, which when supplied are received by a committee comprised of teachers, non teachers and students. This system helps in transparency and quality control.

There is a mess committee meeting held on in every month, headed by the principal. The others members are the vice principal, house masters, associated housemasters, staff nurse, catering assistant, cook, all mess captains, one migrated boy one migrated girl, school captain and the school vice captain. Here all the pros and cons of the mess are discussed and a weekly menu is prepared by the students with the help of the house masters and guided by vice-principal and principal. The menu is exhaustive, varies everyday, which caters the students taste buds and also supplements with full nutrition.


Hostel

The Vidyalaya has its own hostel complex located in its campus which provides free of cost boarding and lodging facilities to all students. Hostels are named on the name of Mountain Ranges as Arawali, Neelgiri, Shivalik and Udaigiri. These hostels are divided into 3 groups, "Junior boys (6th - 8th), Senior boys (9th - 12th) and Girl students". So, there is total of 12 hostels (4 for Jr. boys, 4 for Sr. boys, 2 for Jr. girls and 2 for senior girls). Each Hostel has 2 rooms having 24 beds in each room, and every house is well equipped with electricity, green lawns, 24 hours water supply, clean and well hygined bathrooms and toilet facilities.

There is strict provision for safety and security for students in the vidyalaya. Each hostel is supervised by there House Masters and to associate House master there is a provision for house committee comprised of Associate House Master, House captain and various other members who were responsible for different activities at Vidyalaya to be delivered by the student of the respected house.

For security of girl students there is an extra provision for two "Matrons" who associates respective Hostel's Housemistress. Without matron no girl student is allowed to move outside their hostel weather they wants to go to canteen, mess, academic block, in cultural activities at campus or outside the campus, to meet their parents at campus and other places like playground, morning & evening counting of students. It is duty of matron to bring safely each girl student wherever they goes and report all the activities of the day to Housemistress of the respected house.

For security reasons students are not allowed to leave campus without permission from there House master in the presence of there parents. And if anyone found voilating the rules, then action against them will be taken according to policy regulated by Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti which includes "temporary rustication to permanent suspension of students from the vidyalaya".
