About The Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Hardoi

Establishment

As per the policy of the Government, one Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya is to be established in each district. Accordingly, 62 new JNVs have been sanctioned during 2016-17 in various districts of the country. In addition to this,10 JNVs each have been sanctioned in districts having a large concentration of SC-ST population. Besides this, 3 JNVs have been sanctioned in 03 districts i.e. Senapati-II (Manipur), Ukhrul-II (Manipur) and Ratlam-II (Madhya Pradesh) as special case, bringing the total number of JNVs sanctioned to 661. Each Vidyalaya has provision for a full-fledged campus with sufficient building for classrooms, dormitories, staff-quarters, dining-hall and other infrastructural facilities viz. play-grounds, workshops, library and labs etc. The opening of new Navodaya Vidyalayas is based on the offer from the State Government to provide around 30 acres of suitable land (relaxable on case to case basis) free of cost, alongwith sufficient temporary accommodation to run the school till the completion of permanent Vidyalaya building.


Overview

Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Hardoi, is a fully residential, co-educational school affiliated to CBSE, New Delhi was established in 1992 and has classes from VI to XII standard. This Vidyalaya is run by Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti, New Delhi, an autonomous body under the Department of Education and Literacy, Ministry of Education formely known as Ministry of Human Resource and Development(1985-2020), Government of India.

The school campus covers around 39.8 acres of forested land having various Buildings ( Administration and Academic Block, Hostels, Mess, Staff Quarters, Special Multi-purpose Hall [Under Construction]) and Playgrounds.


Administration

For each JNV, there is a Vidyalaya Advisory Committee for assistance on matters of academics, infrastructure and other general activities and a Vidyalaya Management Committee for budget preparation, selection of ad-hoc teachers and proper functioning of the school.
Normally the district collector of the concerned district is the ex-officio chairman of school level committees with local educationists, public representatives and officers from the district as members.


Academics

Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas are affiliated to Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), New Delhi and follow the curriculum prescribed by CBSE for various classes. The Vidyalaya is having classes from VI to XII and adopts three language system i.e. English, Hindi & Marathi(Regional Language). JNV Hardoi offers Science and Commerce stream for 10+2 students. The medium of instruction for classes VI to VII is Regional Language along with intensive English & Hindi teaching to mould the students to adopt English as medium learning from Class VIII onwards.
The motto of JNV is 'Pragyan Brahma' a Sanskrit phrase which in English means 'Pure Knowledge is God'. P V Narsimha Rao, a distinguished scholar himself conceptualized its design as the then Human Resource Development Minister.

During the morning assembly, students sing a prayer together and also take a pledge which reads “To my country and my people, I pledge my devotion. In their wellbeing and prosperity alone, I pledge my devotion”. The morning assembly ends with the beautiful chorus of students and musical instruments coming together to sing our National Anthem .In addition to academics, JNVs aim to foster and strengthen national integration by ‘student migration policy’. Students of grade nine would go to a JNV of a different state and get exposure of a different society and culture. The three-language formula was an innovative thought ahead of its time along with the co-curricular activities that contributes to the overall development of the student.
At JNV Hardoi there is well equipped Science Labs (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science) and a Samsung Smart Class founded in 2017 having latest technology tools which helps students to learn complicated concepts easily. It also enables teachers in retaining the students’ attention due to this modern and interactive learning environment.

There is a library inside the campus having wide range of unique collection of more than 7000 books covering Education, Encyclopedias, Computer Science, Management, Career Guidence, Psychology, Biographies of eminent personalities, Languages, Literature, School Administration, Teaching and learning process & Evaluation etc. Books are available in both Hindi & English language. The objective of our library is to provide wide range of reading materials to fulfil the needs of our students so they can compete with other students in the country and across the country and benefit society with their knowledge. For the current affairs and competitive exams there is wide range of magzines also availabe in library like: India Today, Outlook, Partiyota Darpan, Kurukshetra, Sport Star, Vigyan Pragati , Biology Today, Chemistry Today, Physics for you, Champak, Suman Saurabh etc. And newspapers like Indian Express, Hindustan Times, Dainik Jagran, Amar Ujala etc. are also available to boost up current affairs.


Mess

Mess is an integral part of JNVs having modern and clean kitchen which provides free of cost fooding facility to all students.The food court kitchen is equipped with highly sophisticated cooking equipment, provides hygienic, delicious and wholesome food, prepared under the direction of well qualified and experienced catering staff. It has a common dinning hall, for boys, girls and teachers. The ambience of the mess is so quite. Students used to do "Bhojan Mantra" before having the meal.

The dining hall is well ventilated, decorated and delightfully arranged. Audio visual equipments are also fitted in the hall to entertain the students as well as the information update. There is a tender system for the procurement of raw materials. Indents are placed to the suppliers well in advance, which when supplied are received by a committee comprised of teachers, non teachers and students. This system helps in transparency and quality control.

There is a mess committee meeting held on in every month, headed by the principal. The others members are the vice principal, house masters, associated housemasters, staff nurse, catering assistant, cook, all mess captains, one migrated boy one migrated girl, school captain and the school vice captain. Here all the pros and cons of the mess are discussed and a weekly menu is prepared by the students with the help of the house masters and guided by vice-principal and principal. The menu is exhaustive, varies everyday, which caters the students taste buds and also supplements with full nutrition.


Hostel

The Vidyalaya has its own hostel complex located in its campus which provides free of cost boarding and lodging facilities to all students. Hostels are named on the name of Mountain Ranges as Arawali, Neelgiri, Shivalik and Udaigiri. These hostels are divided into 3 groups, "Junior boys (6th - 8th), Senior boys (9th - 12th) and Girl students". So, there is total of 12 hostels (4 for Jr. boys, 4 for Sr. boys, 2 for Jr. girls and 2 for senior girls). Each Hostel has 2 rooms having 24 beds in each room, and every house is well equipped with electricity, green lawns, 24 hours water supply, clean and well hygined bathrooms and toilet facilities.

There is strict provision for safety and security for students in the vidyalaya. Each hostel is supervised by there House Masters and to associate House master there is a provision for house committee comprised of Associate House Master, House captain and various other members who were responsible for different activities at Vidyalaya to be delivered by the student of the respected house.

For security of girl students there is an extra provision for two "Matrons" who associates respective Hostel's Housemistress. Without matron no girl student is allowed to move outside their hostel weather they wants to go to canteen, mess, academic block, in cultural activities at campus or outside the campus, to meet their parents at campus and other places like playground, morning & evening counting of students. It is duty of matron to bring safely each girl student wherever they goes and report all the activities of the day to Housemistress of the respected house.

For security reasons students are not allowed to leave campus without permission from there House master in the presence of there parents. And if anyone found voilating the rules, then action against them will be taken according to policy regulated by Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti which includes "temporary rustication to permanent suspension of students from the vidyalaya".


Sports

Games and Sports have been a vital component of JNVs to ensure all round development of students. The importance of sports and games in schools encompasses more than just the benefit of physical activity. It not only helps to improve academic performance but it is also a powerful tool for promoting leadership, teamwork skills, and social cohesion. Games and Sports are encouraged in JNVs to develop fitness, psychomotor skills and fine-tune motor skills with coordination, movement, strength, dexterity, grace, speed skills and to promote the spirit of cooperation and sportsman. Apart from the same, young talents are identified at an early stage and are helped to excel in their chosen field by specialized coaching. Further, Games and Sports also find healthy outlets for energy. This promotes a good rapport among students, teachers, parents and administration and showcase talents of students for the inspiration of others. Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti organizes various sports and games activities at the school level, cluster level, Regional Level and National Level every year. Further Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti is a state under School Games Federation of India (SGFI) and Navodaya National teams compete with other state teams in SGFI competitions.

JNV Hardoi has various playgrounds and courts (seprate for boys and girls) of most of popular games like Cricket, Football, Volleyball, Basketball, Handball, Badminton, Table-Tennis, Kabaddi, Kho-Kho and various other athletic activities for which there is 400m track at campus. JNV hardoi students played SGFI in atheletics and National in various other games and activities. JNV hardoi also hosted National Level Football and Kabbadi competition.


Cultural Activities

Cultural activities ensures the harmonious growth of personality in children to make the children aware, and nurture art and cultural heritage and to develop the values of national Integration, a strong component of culture has been provided for JNV students by organising different kinds of performing art, craft, visual art, music and theatre workshops each year. The vidyalya has got rich department of art and music which offers a great opportunity to students to learn various kind of art, music, drama, community songs and dances etc. Vidyalaya promotes sense of belonginess towards nature and mother earth thorugh many events by organising many competitions on various occasions like world Environment day, World health day and many more.

The main cultural programs at JNV Hardoi are Republic Day,Independence Day, Teacher's Day, Children's Day and Krishna Janmastami. Celebration of Krishna Janmastami is the most exciting cultural program at JNV Hardoi, for the celebration students start prepare themselves 7-10 days before the event and on the day they beautifully portrays the various glimpses of "Lord Krishana's" life that inspires each and every soul throughout the life. And another exciting program is Teacher's Day. On the day the 12th class students portrays the role of all the teachers and the day brings an opportunity for students to see the class from a teacher's point of view and they learn a lifetime lesson by taking all the responsibilities of teachers and the day reminds them why the teachers nag them for an ill practice. The day give them a life lesson that "without teachers no one can succeed in life".
